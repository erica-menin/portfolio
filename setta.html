Project Brief

As for our final project on the Bootcamp, Domenique and I had the amazing opportunity to work with a company that is thinking about people’s mental health and how they can improve their lives by enjoying it the most.

Roles: We were both heavily involved in the end-to-end design process which included research, synthesis, interface design, testing and prototyping.

Our Client

Setta is a company focused on personal development and is based in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Their mission is support and motivate users in their pursuit of personal development and well being.

They are about to launch their own app with the goal to keep helping people in this pursuit of a healthier and happier life, by motivating them to create new habits.

The Challenge

Setta is a company focused on personal development and is based in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Their mission is support and motivate users in their pursuit of personal development and well being.

They are about to launch their own app with the goal to keep helping people in this pursuit of a healthier and happier life, by motivating them to create new habits.

Goals

  • To make the user naturally scroll down through the pages, offering a pleasant and engaging experience;
  • To attract different users – including those that might not be aware that they could use the product;
  • To convince users to sign up for the waiting list, so they could download the app as soon as it was launched

Research

After our kickoff meeting with our stakeholders, it was time to research. To empathise not only with our potential users but also with Setta and their philosophy. To do so, we split the research into 5 different parts:

Existing Research

Setta kindly shared an existing research made by another agency, where they were investigating the company and redefining some aspects of them as a brand.

That was great to understand them better and dive deeper into some aspects like the scenario we were dealing with, their internal perception and to start to understand their audience and competitors.

Interviews

For the interviews, we decided to focus on two different groups of people:

  • Those ones recommended by the company: potential users (mostly Brazilians) in their late twenties or early thirties that are not really satisfied with their lives in general or are feeling stressed. They might know that there’s something wrong, but they are not really aware that investing in personal development could help them.
  • People that have searched for personal development before: and are still into it. Why? To understand when was the click moment that made them starting to change and what motivated them to do so.

Interviews' Insights

Group 1

  • The pressure to achieve a high performance comes from school
  • This makes them being motivated by challenges. But...
  • This put them under a lot of pressure, resulting in stress
  • Most of the stress is created by the user themselves
  • Users are aware they could improve their lives
  • A few have tried to do so, but nothing really motivates them

Group 2

  • Have created new habits after realising something was wrong
  • It took time and dedication to start to see results
  • The change hasn't come only with meditation apps
  • They kept their new habits and try to keep evolving
  • Positive attitude and new habits were essential for them to change

Survey

Our goal was to try to identify what was attractive to users when we were talking about landing pages.

  • What is important?
  • What keeps them engaged?
  • What makes them scroll down the page?

We had around 80 answers that showed us that besides images and illustrations, some important elements to have are a product preview; information about the company, interactive elements (like quizzes or games) and testimonials from real people.

Subscription Economy

We also researched the best practices on the subscription economy. Here we checked what is really working out there and combined our findings with the user-centered approach. Here are a few examples:

  • Focus on the customer and their personalised experience
  • Show that the product works, make the product benefits clear
  • Write like a human: speak the user’s language
  • Use freemium versions to acquire customers
  • Client reviews and opinions are an important factor of trust

Competitors

We made a competitor research and comparison — also checking their landing pages: the language they use, if they show app’s features, offer a freemium version, have testimonials from real people and so on.

From there, we developed a Market Positioning Map where we show the gap in the market and the potential and ideal position for Setta to be.

Setta is a “one-shop-stop”, where users will find more than spiritual and meditation guidance or suggestions on how to relax. They will go further. And all based on practices and habits that have been tested with real people.

Defining

We gathered all the research insights by creating an Affinity Diagram in Mural, which helped us to come up with ideas later on.

By clustering similar ideas, we were able to:

  • Confirm the problem we were trying to solve
  • Understand who our users are
      • What are their motivations?
      • What are their beliefs and mindset?
      • What do they need? (not what do they want)
      • How do they behave?
      • Why?

By answering these questions, we created our User Persona.

Meet Caio

Caio used to be the one whose presence lighted up the room.
But something has been changing. Even though he went to a great school, has a great job and is one of the best employees of his company… He feels that there’s something missing.

He’s always been driven by challenges and has never found a bad time to achieve his goals. But now he is feeling lost. He drives to work every day wishing he had more: more money, time, enjoyment. A more balanced life.

He knows he needs to change, but he doesn't know how. He feels stuck.

Brainstorming

We started to create HMW questions that could be the seeds to bloom our solution. After discussing the paths we could follow on our landing page, we brainstormed our users’ awakening process — which was defined in a 3-step guide.

Basically, we would have to make the users:

  1. Stop and pay attention to the page
  2. Reflect on the power of the topic we are talking about
  3. Realise it can help them (signing up the waiting list)

Time to think about the structure

With these ideas in mind, it was time to start to think about the structure of our landing pages. We looked back at our subscription economy research and made a MOSCOW map based on market best practices.

We decided that we should focus on:

  • Feature previews
  • Personalised content
  • Offering something in order to have emails

Wireframes

We sketched the first screens together on InVision Freehand. From there, we were ready to build our first mid-fis wireframes. We created 3 pages that would be tested with different people. After collecting feedback, we understood we had to adapt the language and the structure. We also decided to keep working on 2 out of the 3 pages – using different strategies to attract different kinds of users.

We named them as:

  1. Goal-oriented: focused on people who are extremely dedicated to their jobs and that pressure themselves to achieve high performance at all times;
  2. Emotional: focused on people that are emotionally driven; feelings topics would call their attention.

Design

By now we were ready to start to work on the interface design.

Setta is in a process where they are changing the whole brand, including logo and colours they use. They kindly shared with us their new brand guidelines, and we used their imagery to create a moodboard.

We want the users to feel calm, light and free

Style Tiles

Thinking about that, we created the style tiles that were the basis for our final design. Here we kept the fonts, colours, gradients, imagery and illustrations styles that were on the brand guidelines.

Feedback meeting

This time, we presented our design to Setta’s COO. We also developed all the copywriting – as an extra – and she was amazed by that — clearly satisfied with the results so far.

We got some feedback from her and then we iterate once again:

  • Colour adjustments
  • Image sizes
  • CTA’s content

The Grand Finale

We presented our project on the remote Hackshow — where more than 70 people attended, including Setta’s members. That’s one of the greatest advantages of working remotely — it doesn’t matter where we are, we can connect people from different locations.

By the end of the presentation, we were surprised by Setta’s CEO — we weren’t sure if he would have time to watch it. He said that they would definitely use our pages and that it was a great way to start his day by watching our presentation.
We couldn’t be more thrilled—there’s nothing more exciting than seeing our client happy with the result.

Summary

What a journey! There were incredible 10 days where I kept learning with my partner, stakeholders and the whole process. The main lessons I get from the experience are:

  • Research. Research more. And again. I am super proud of our extensive research and I recognise all the benefits of investing the time on that. By dedicating extra time to the research we ended up saving time in the end. We also shared everything with the company and they seemed very happy with that. Hopefully, it will give them as many insights and it gave us!
  • Cultural differences. I am Brazilian myself, but I haven’t thought that some things would be harder to be understood by my German partner, just because we come from different backgrounds. And this applies to other scenarios other than coming from different countries. We are not our users. We have to understand who they are! And this is one of the greatest things about our job, in my opinion.
